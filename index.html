About the database

Bridging the data gap in avian disease ecology

Wildlife diseases are far less understood than human diseases, partly due to the lack of structured data on host-parasite interactions. The GBPD addresses this gap for avian systems.

Global scope

Records span all continents and major avian orders, compiled from primary literature across decades of field research.

Structured records

Each interaction is recorded with 29 standardised variables including taxonomy, geography, sampling method, and infection metrics.

Community-driven

Researchers can submit missing records or papers for inclusion. All submissions are reviewed before integration.

Scope

Parasites covered

The GBPD includes records across all major parasite groups infecting wild birds, including but not limited to:

Haemosporidians Helminths Ectoparasites Bacteria Viruses Cimicids Protozoa Fungi Nematodes Trematodes
